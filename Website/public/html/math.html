HiMCM: Math Modeling Contest

The High School Mathematical Contest in Modeling is a math competition that took place over the course of multiple days. The competition challenged us to research a given problem and write a paper detailing our own solution to that problem within a relatively short time frame. I worked alongside Simon Beyzerov, Gracie Sheng, and Rhik Mazumder to tackle Problem B, which concerned declining water levels in the Lake Mead reservoir. Our primary rationale for choosing this problem over the other one was simply that we perceived it to involve more robust usage of mathematics.

Median-Median Line

An integral part of our presentational toolkit is a program called Mathematica, developed by Wolfram-Alpha. Mathematica features its own “programming language” and is capable of both markdown document formatting and complex computations. To practice writing solutions in Mathematica, we implemented a linear regression algorithm called median-median to calculate a line of best fit given a dataset of (x, y) coordinates.
