Physics, taught by Mr. Ellis, is a healthy balance between fundamental concepts and real-world application. In the first semester, we covered kinematics, forces, energy, and momentum. I had a basic understanding of these topics before coming into this course, but the practice I gained robustified my knowledge. We are currently working on rotational motion—which I knew nothing about—but it seems to be an analog of translational motion thus far.

Über Rocket Calculus

Über problems are multi-stage physics problems that cover a wide range of topics discussed in the unit. In the kinematics unit, we tracked the motion of a rocket launched in 1D space with nonlinear acceleration. The end goal was simply to find the total time elapsed before the rocket reached the ground, but it was necessary to componentize the problem into the different stages of the rocket’s motion.

Rock and Balloon

To practice formatting Excel documents, we plotted the height of a rock and balloon against time given a few initial values. The exercise demonstrated that an entire table of data could be rapidly calculated by representing physics equations on the computer and using Excel shortcuts. We also learned to properly format graphs and space elements on the page.
